Quick Tips

💨

Pursed-Lip Breathing

Breathe in through your nose for 2 counts, then out through pursed lips for 4. Slows breathing and reduces shortness of breath.

🚶

Pace Yourself

Slow down activities and take breaks. Exerting too fast triggers breathlessness — pacing helps you do more with less strain.

💧

Stay Hydrated

Drinking enough water keeps mucus thin and easier to clear from your airways throughout the day.

🌬️

Avoid Triggers

Smoke, dust, strong perfumes, and cold air can worsen symptoms. Identify your personal triggers and minimize exposure.

💊

Use Inhalers Correctly

Technique matters. Ask your doctor or pharmacist to review your inhaler method — improper use reduces medication effectiveness.

📅

Track Your Symptoms

Keep a simple daily log of breathlessness and energy. Patterns help you and your doctor catch worsening early.

COPD Basics

What Is COPD and Why Does It Get Worse Over Time?

COPD (Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease) is a long-term lung condition that causes airflow blockage, making it harder to breathe. Understanding the progression helps patients and caregivers plan ahead.

Exercise

Gentle Exercise Routines Safe for COPD Patients

Regular physical activity can improve exercise tolerance and quality of life even with COPD. Walking, chair exercises, and pulmonary rehab programs are proven options.

Nutrition

Eating Well With COPD: Foods That Support Lung Health

A balanced diet with anti-inflammatory foods — leafy greens, fatty fish, and fruits — may help reduce inflammation and support better breathing.

Daily Life

How to Manage a COPD Flare-Up at Home

Knowing your action plan before a flare-up starts is critical. Learn the warning signs and steps to take so you can respond quickly and avoid hospitalization.
